Essential AI Tools & Learning Resources

Curated links to the best AI assistants, prompting guides, and safety frameworks for knowledge workers.

AI Assistants
Claude
Anthropic's AI assistant. Strong for long documents, nuanced analysis, and careful reasoning. Recommended for professional and sensitive work.
ChatGPT
OpenAI's widely-used assistant. Large plugin and GPT ecosystem. Strong general-purpose capabilities with image generation via DALL-E.
Gemini
Google's AI model. Integrates with Google Workspace (Docs, Sheets, Gmail). Best choice if your organization runs on Google tools.
Perplexity
AI search with real-time web access and inline citations. Best for research tasks where source traceability matters.
Microsoft Copilot
AI built into Microsoft 365 and Windows. Best for organizations on the Microsoft stack — Word, Excel, Outlook, Teams.
NotebookLM
Google's research assistant. Upload your own documents and have AI answer questions based solely on your sources — great for grounded research.
Prompting & Learning
Prompt Engineering Guide
Comprehensive, research-backed guide to prompting techniques: zero-shot, few-shot, chain-of-thought, RAG, and more. Updated regularly.
Learn Prompting
Free open-source course covering practical prompting from basics to advanced techniques. Community-maintained with examples across many use cases.
OpenAI Prompt Engineering Guide
Official guide from OpenAI with six strategies for getting better results. Practical examples for clarity, context, and structured outputs.
Standards & Safety
NIST AI Resources
The US government's AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF 1.0) and related standards. The most widely cited framework for enterprise AI governance.
Anthropic Research
Published research on AI safety, interpretability, and responsible scaling. Useful for understanding how frontier models are evaluated and constrained.
OpenAI Research
OpenAI's published papers on model capabilities, alignment, and safety evaluations. Includes the GPT-4 technical report and usage policy documentation.
