R Quick Reference

Reference & Resources

Glossary of AI terms, reusable prompt templates you can copy immediately, and curated links to the best tools and learning resources.

Agent
An AI system that can plan and take actions autonomously, using tools like web search or code execution to complete multi-step tasks with minimal oversight.
Artifact
The specific output you're asking AI to produce: a document, email, table, summary, or other deliverable. Defining it clearly before prompting improves quality.
Chain-of-thought
A prompting technique that asks the AI to reason through a problem step by step before giving a final answer. Improves accuracy on complex or multi-part tasks.
Context window
The maximum amount of text a model can process in one interaction—your input plus its output combined. Most frontier models now handle 100,000+ tokens.
Embedding
A numerical representation of text that captures semantic meaning. Used to power semantic search, document retrieval, and similarity matching in AI systems.
Few-shot prompting
Providing 2–5 examples inside your prompt to show the model the exact format, tone, or structure you want in the output. More reliable than describing the format in words.
Fine-tuning
Training an existing model on a curated dataset to specialize its behavior for a particular domain, task, or style. Requires technical infrastructure and labeled data.
Foundation model
A large AI model trained on broad data (like Claude, GPT-4, or Gemini) that can be applied to many tasks without retraining. The base most AI products are built on.
Grounding
Connecting AI responses to specific verified documents or data sources. Reduces hallucination by anchoring the model to facts rather than relying on training alone.
Hallucination
When an AI confidently generates false, fabricated, or inaccurate information that sounds plausible. The most important failure mode to verify against before using AI output.
Inference
The process of an AI model generating a response from its training. Inference cost and speed vary by model size—larger models are slower and more expensive to run.
LLM
Large Language Model. An AI model trained on vast amounts of text to understand and generate natural language. The core technology behind most conversational AI tools.
Multimodal
AI systems that can process multiple input types—text, images, audio, or video—in a single model. GPT-4o, Claude, and Gemini are all multimodal models.
Prompt
The instruction, question, or input you give to an AI model to guide its response. Prompt quality is the primary lever for output quality in everyday AI use.
RAG
Retrieval-Augmented Generation. Relevant documents are fetched and added to the AI's context before it responds, grounding answers in real data rather than training memory.
System prompt
Instructions set before a conversation begins that shape the AI's behavior, persona, or constraints throughout the session. Used in products to define tone and scope.
Temperature
A parameter controlling output randomness. Low values produce focused, consistent responses. High values produce more varied or creative ones. Most tools set this automatically.
Token
The basic unit an LLM processes—roughly ¾ of a word on average. Context limits, API costs, and processing speed are all measured in tokens.
Tool use
The ability of an AI model to call external functions—web search, code execution, calendar access, APIs—during a conversation to retrieve live information or take action.
Zero-shot
Asking an AI to perform a task without providing any examples, relying entirely on the instruction and its training. Works well for common tasks; few-shot helps for unusual formats.
Universal
Starter Template
Help me with this task: [describe the task clearly] Goal: [what a good result looks like] Audience: [who will read or use this] Format: [how you want the output structured] Constraints: [anything it must or must not include]
Universal
Structured Complex Request
[TASK] [describe the task in one sentence] [CONTEXT] Audience: [who will use this] Background: [relevant context] Source material: [what to use as input] [REQUIREMENTS] Must include: [required elements or sections] Must not include: [exclusions or constraints] Format: [desired output structure] [SUCCESS CRITERIA] A good result will: [what makes it successful]
Writing
Draft a Message
Write a [formal/friendly/brief] [email/memo/update] to [audience] about [topic]. Key points to cover: - [point 1] - [point 2] - [point 3] Tone: [professional/warm/direct] Length: Under [X] words. Do not include: [anything to avoid]
Writing
Rewrite for a Different Audience
Rewrite this message for [new audience]. They care about: [their priorities] What they already know: [shared context] What they need to do next: [required action or decision] Tone: [appropriate tone for this audience] Original: [paste message here]
Writing
Polish Existing Text
Improve the clarity of this text. Keep the meaning intact. Flag anything that is vague, could be misread, weakens the point, or is unnecessarily long. Text: [paste text here]
Meetings
Meeting Recap
Turn these meeting notes into a structured recap. Include: 1. Key decisions made (with brief context) 2. Action items (owner and deadline where mentioned) 3. Open questions or unresolved items 4. Recommended next steps Notes: [paste notes here]
Research
Research Brief
I need to understand [topic] for [specific purpose]. Give me: 1. The core concept in plain English (2–3 sentences) 2. Three key things I need to know 3. Main risks or misconceptions to watch for 4. One question I should ask before relying on this I am not a technical expert — keep it practical.
Research
Action-Oriented Summary
Summarize this document for [audience] who needs to [make a decision / take action]. Include: - The key finding in one sentence - What it means for [audience] - Recommended next step - What is still uncertain or unknown Source: [paste document here]
Analysis
Compare Options
Compare [option A] and [option B] for [use case]. Create a comparison table with these criteria: - [criterion 1] - [criterion 2] - [criterion 3] Then give a recommendation based on: [my priorities or constraints]. Note any trade-offs I should think through before deciding.
Analysis
Risk Check
Review this [plan/proposal/decision] and identify: 1. Risks I might have missed 2. Assumptions that could be wrong 3. Questions a skeptic would raise 4. One thing to verify before moving forward [paste here]
AI Assistants
Claude
Anthropic's AI assistant. Strong for long documents, nuanced analysis, and careful reasoning. Recommended for professional and sensitive work.
ChatGPT
OpenAI's widely-used assistant. Large plugin and GPT ecosystem. Strong general-purpose capabilities with image generation via DALL-E.
Gemini
Google's AI model. Integrates with Google Workspace (Docs, Sheets, Gmail). Best choice if your organization runs on Google tools.
Perplexity
AI search with real-time web access and inline citations. Best for research tasks where source traceability matters.
Microsoft Copilot
AI built into Microsoft 365 and Windows. Best for organizations on the Microsoft stack — Word, Excel, Outlook, Teams.
NotebookLM
Google's research assistant. Upload your own documents and have AI answer questions based solely on your sources — great for grounded research.
Prompting & Learning
Prompt Engineering Guide
Comprehensive, research-backed guide to prompting techniques: zero-shot, few-shot, chain-of-thought, RAG, and more. Updated regularly.
Learn Prompting
Free open-source course covering practical prompting from basics to advanced techniques. Community-maintained with examples across many use cases.
OpenAI Prompt Engineering Guide
Official guide from OpenAI with six strategies for getting better results. Practical examples for clarity, context, and structured outputs.
Standards & Safety
NIST AI Resources
The US government's AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF 1.0) and related standards. The most widely cited framework for enterprise AI governance.
Anthropic Research
Published research on AI safety, interpretability, and responsible scaling. Useful for understanding how frontier models are evaluated and constrained.
OpenAI Research
OpenAI's published papers on model capabilities, alignment, and safety evaluations. Includes the GPT-4 technical report and usage policy documentation.
